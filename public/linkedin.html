About the Role

Verdant Agritech is seeking a motivated and detail-oriented Cultivar Development Technician to join our crop science team at our Trafford Park facility in Greater Manchester. This is a hands-on research and operations role supporting the cultivation, monitoring, and iterative improvement of Verdant's proprietary bioenergy crop programme.

The successful candidate will work closely with our Senior Agronomists and the Crop Systems team to maintain optimal growing conditions across our controlled environment units, contribute to ongoing phenotypic observation protocols, and support the data collection processes that underpin Verdant's continuous cultivar refinement pipeline.

This is an exciting opportunity to work at the forefront of applied agricultural biotechnology, contributing to a programme with real significance for the UK's domestic energy security landscape.

Key Responsibilities

  • Day-to-day husbandry and environmental management of cultivar stock across controlled growing environments and open-field trial plots
  • Systematic phenotypic observation and documentation of growth, structural development, and autonomous response behaviours, in accordance with approved protocols
  • Collection, labelling, and processing of biological samples for internal analysis; maintenance of accurate chain-of-custody records
  • Monitoring and reporting of environmental parameters (light exposure, temperature, humidity, soil composition) using Verdant's proprietary sensor suite
  • Supporting containment and perimeter integrity checks across growing units; escalation of anomalous observations to senior staff per standard operating procedures
  • Contributing to periodic cultivar assessment reviews and detailed incident logs
  • Assisting with the safe handling, transfer, and controlled disposal of biological material under the supervision of the Site Safety Officer

Essential Requirements

  • BSc or equivalent qualification in Plant Biology, Agricultural Science, Botany, Bioengineering, or a closely related discipline
  • Demonstrable practical experience in controlled environment agriculture, laboratory plant science, or fieldwork in a professional or extended academic context
  • Familiarity with systematic observation methodologies and scientific record-keeping standards
  • Physical fitness appropriate to outdoor and semi-outdoor fieldwork; comfort working in varying weather conditions
  • A methodical and patient approach to repetitive observation tasks; high attention to detail
  • Full UK driving licence

Desirable

  • Experience with autonomous or semi-autonomous plant response monitoring
  • Familiarity with oleaginous crop biology or industrial bioenergy feedstock systems
  • Previous exposure to biocontainment protocols or GMO handling procedures
  • IOSH Working Safely or equivalent H&S qualification

About Verdant Agritech

Verdant Agritech is a UK-based agricultural biotechnology company and a wholly owned subsidiary of Vale Group plc. Founded in 2013, Verdant specialises in the development, cultivation, and agronomic optimisation of proprietary bioenergy crop systems, with a focus on high-yield, low-input cultivars suited to the UK's temperate growing conditions and marginal land types.

Our primary cultivar programme produces the feedstock for Verdant's downstream processing and refinery operations, supplying the raw biological material that underpins the VeraFuel product range — Vale Group's consumer and commercial biofuel brand, available at over 400 forecourts nationwide.

Verdant operates a network of cultivation sites, processing facilities, and controlled environment research units across England and Wales, with research partnerships at several UK agricultural institutions. We are expanding our footprint in response to growing demand for domestically sourced bioenergy feedstock and remain committed to advancing the science of high-performance crop development in support of the UK's net-zero transition.

Our Goals

Verdant Agritech's long-term mission is to demonstrate that the UK can meet a significant proportion of its liquid fuel needs from domestically grown, sustainably managed biological sources — reducing dependence on imported fossil fuels, creating skilled employment in rural and post-industrial areas, and building a resilient, home-grown energy supply chain for the decades ahead.

Our cultivar development programme is central to this mission. By continuously refining the agronomic performance, yield characteristics, and environmental adaptability of our proprietary crop lines, we are building a foundation for bioenergy production that is genuinely scalable, genuinely sustainable, and genuinely British.
